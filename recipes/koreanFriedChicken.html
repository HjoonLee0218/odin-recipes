Korean Fried Chicken

Description

Korean Fried Chicken, often abbreviated as KFC, is a delectable culinary creation that has gained international popularity. Renowned for its irresistibly crispy and crunchy exterior, the dish features bite-sized pieces of chicken coated in a flavorful and often slightly spicy batter. What sets Korean Fried Chicken apart is its dual frying process, which ensures a remarkably crispy texture while maintaining moist and tender meat on the inside. The fried chicken is typically served with an array of savory sauces, ranging from sweet and sticky to tangy and spicy, further enhancing its indulgent appeal. This beloved dish has become a symbol of Korean cuisine, celebrated for its impeccable balance of textures and unforgettable taste.

Ingredients

Chicken
  • 1.4kg/3lbs whole chicken cut into pieces or chicken wings / drumsticks / thigh
  • 2 Tbsp rice wine
  • 2 tsp minced ginger
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1/2 tsp ground black pepper
  • 1 cup potato or corn starch
  • Cooking oil for frying
Sauce
  • 4 Tbsp corn syrup
  • 2 Tbsp ketchup
  • 2 Tbsp minced garlic
  • 2 Tbsp sugar
  • 1 Tbsp gochujang
  • 1 Tbsp water
  • 1 Tbsp soy sauce

Steps

  1. Place the chicken, rice wine, ginger, salt and black pepper in a ball and combine well. Then evenly coat the chicken with the starch.
  2. Add a generous amount of oil in a pan and heat it until the oil temperature reaches 175 C / 347 F. Fry the chicken until they are cooked and set them aside on a paper towel.
  3. Once the oil reaches 175 C / 347 F again, fry them again until they are golden crispy
  4. Add the sauce ingredients to a pan and mix. Heat it up until it starts to boil and serve.
